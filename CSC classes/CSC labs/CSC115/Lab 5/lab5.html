1. Queues

  • Queue operations:
    • enqueue(item) --- adds the item to the back of the queue (does NOT affect items in front).
    • dequeue() --- removes the item at the front of the queue and returns it.
    • peek() --- returns the item at the front of the queue (but does not change the contents of the queue).
    • isEmpty() --- returns true if the queue is empty, false otherwise.
    • dequeueAll() --- removes all items from the queue.
  • How does this work if we want to store our items in an array?
    Assume we have an array that can hold 4 items.
    What will the array look like after the following operations?
    • enqueue(3); enqueue(5); enqueue(8);
    • dequeue(); enqueue(2); dequeue();
    • How do we keep track of items at the front of the queue vs the back? What are ways of implementing this?
    • dequeue(); enqueue(1); enqueue(3); enqueue(7); dequeue(); enqueue(9);

First, let's take a look at the TokenList.java file
Infix-to-postfix algorithm (page 375 to 378 in the textbook):
  1. When you encounter an operand (#), append it to the postFixTokens TokenList.
  2. Push each "(" onto the stack
  3. When you encounter an operator (+, -, etc), if the stack is empty, push it onto the stack
    • If the stack is not empty, pop operators of greater or equal precedence from the stack and append them to the postFixTokens TokenList.
    • Stop when you encounter an operator with lower precedence, a "(", or the stack is empty.
    • Push the new operator onto the stack.
  4. When you encounter a ")", pop operators off the stack and append them to postFixTokens until you find a "(" on the stack.
  5. When you reach the end of the input, pop the remaining items from the stack and append them to postFixTokens.
